# MSR Multiple Valuation — Overview

**Mortgage Servicing Rights (MSRs)** entitle the servicer to collect servicing fees (typically 25 bps for conventional, 44 bps for GNMA) over the life of the loan in exchange for performing servicing duties.

## What is the MSR Multiple?
- **MSR Multiple = MSR Value (% of UPB) ÷ Servicing Fee Rate (%)**
- Example: MSR trading at 1.25% of UPB with a 25 bp servicing fee = **5.0x multiple**.
- Typical range: **3.0x – 6.0x** depending on rate environment, loan characteristics, and portfolio quality.
- Higher multiple = more valuable MSR (longer expected life, lower cost to service, higher ancillary income).

## Core Valuation Drivers

The multiple boils down to the **present value of net future cash flows** from servicing. Every characteristic below either extends the expected life of the cash flow, increases the cash flow, or reduces the risk/cost of collecting it.
