# Loan Age and Seasoning

Age of the loan (WALA – Weighted Average Loan Age) affects both prepay behavior and remaining cash flow duration.

## Seasoning Effects
- **Ramp-up (0–30 months)**: Prepay speeds ramp per PSA curve; new loans prepay slower.
- **Peak refi window (12–48 months)**: If in the money, refi speeds peak here.
- **Burnout**: Loans that have survived multiple refi opportunities without refinancing prepay slower — borrowers may be credit-impaired, unaware, or intentionally holding.

## Multiple Impact
- **Very new loans (WALA < 6 mo)**: High multiple upside if OOTM, but full remaining term at risk.
- **Seasoned & burned out**: Often carry premium multiples due to demonstrated stickiness.
- **Old loans with low remaining balance**: Cost-to-service becomes proportionally high, hurting multiple.
