# Regulatory and Compliance Risk

Regulatory environment affects both cost and permissible practices.

## Federal Oversight
- **CFPB**: Servicing rules (Reg X / RESPA amendments) — force-placed insurance, loss mit, error resolution, periodic statements.
- **HUD / FHA**: FHA servicing standards, HERMIT for HECM.
- **VA / USDA**: Product-specific rules.
- **FHFA**: Sets Enterprise servicer eligibility standards (min net worth, liquidity, capital).

## State Oversight
- State licensing required in every state where loans serviced.
- Some states (NY DFS, CA DFPI, WA DFI) very aggressive on servicing exams.
- Onerous states raise compliance cost, may deter buyers.

## Recent Rule Changes
- Post-COVID: forbearance handling, loss-mit waterfalls updated.
- CFPB's servicing rule "streamlined loss mit" changes.

## Impact on Multiple
- Heavier regulatory burden → higher cost → lower multiple.
- Reputational risk: mishandling → enforcement action → tail risk.
- Consent orders / MRAs on servicer can materially discount MSR value.
