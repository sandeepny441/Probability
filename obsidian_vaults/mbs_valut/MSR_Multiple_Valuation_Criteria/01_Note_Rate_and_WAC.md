# Note Rate and WAC (Weighted Average Coupon)

The single most important characteristic driving MSR value alongside prepayment behavior.

## Why It Matters
- **Note rate vs. prevailing mortgage rate** determines refinance incentive.
- If WAC >> current market rate → borrower is **"in the money"** to refinance → high prepay risk → **lower multiple**.
- If WAC << current market rate → borrower is **"out of the money"** → low prepay → **higher multiple**.

## Multiple Impact
- Deeply in-the-money MSRs (WAC 200+ bps above market): multiple can compress to **2.0x–3.0x**.
- At-the-money: **4.0x–5.0x**.
- Deeply out-of-the-money (WAC 150+ bps below market): multiple can extend to **5.5x–6.5x+**.

## Related Metric
- **SATO (Spread at Origination)**: Note rate vs. prevailing rate *at origination* — proxy for borrower credit quality.
