# Escrow Balances and Float

Escrow accounts hold funds for property taxes and insurance.

## Float Income
- Servicer earns interest on escrow balances (in most states; some require pass-through to borrower).
- Float income = escrow balance × short-term rate.
- In **high rate environments**, float income can add **0.5x–1.0x** to MSR multiple.
- In near-zero rate environments (2020-21), float income near zero.

## Impact on Valuation
- Escrowed loans (typical for high-LTV, first-time buyers, GNMA) more valuable to servicer.
- Non-escrowed loans (low-LTV refis, jumbo) offer no float income.

## Related State Rules
- CA, CT, IA, MA, MD, MN, NH, NJ, NY, RI, UT, VT, WI: require interest paid on escrow.
