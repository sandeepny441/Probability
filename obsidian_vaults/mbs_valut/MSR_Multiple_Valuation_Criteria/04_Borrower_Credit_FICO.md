# Borrower Credit (FICO)

FICO score at origination — and updated FICO — affects both prepay and default risk.

## Prepay Effect
- **High FICO (740+)**: Sophisticated, rate-aware, faster refis when ITM → lower multiple in refi environments.
- **Low FICO (< 680)**: Slower to refinance (may not qualify), stickier → higher multiple *if* delinquency is managed.

## Default Effect
- Lower FICO → higher default probability → more advances, higher servicing cost, more loss severity.
- MSR loses value when loan defaults (advances required, eventual buyout).

## Optimal Sweet Spot
- Mid-FICO borrowers (680–720) often provide **best MSR economics**: not fast refinancers, not high default risk.
