# Discount Rate Assumptions

MSR is valued as PV of future net cash flows — discount rate is critical.

## Discount Rate Components
- **Risk-free rate**: Treasury / swap curve.
- **Option-adjusted spread (OAS)**: Compensation for prepay/extension risk.
- **Liquidity premium**: MSRs are illiquid vs. TBAs.
- **Operational risk premium**: Servicing execution risk.

## Typical Levels
- **Agency MSR discount rates**: 8–12% (varies by cycle).
- **GNMA MSR**: 10–14% (higher risk).
- **Delinquent MSR / non-agency**: 15%+.

## Sensitivity
- +100 bps discount rate → multiple falls ~0.3–0.5x.
- Different market participants use different rates:
  - **Banks**: Lower (cheap funding, hedge in balance sheet).
  - **Non-banks / REITs**: Higher (equity cost of capital).
  - **PE funds**: Higher still (IRR targets 15%+).

## Practical Note
- Two participants can look at same MSR and value it very differently based on their discount rate assumptions.
