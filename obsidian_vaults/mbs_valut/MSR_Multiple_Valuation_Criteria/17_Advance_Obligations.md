# Advance Obligations

Servicer must advance funds when borrowers don't pay — creates working capital drag.

## Types of Advances
- **P&I Advances**: Scheduled principal & interest to security holders (agency guidelines vary).
- **T&I Advances**: Property tax and insurance from escrow shortfalls.
- **Corporate Advances**: Foreclosure attorney fees, property preservation, inspections.

## Agency Rules
- **FNMA**: Scheduled/scheduled — advance until reasonably nonrecoverable (typically 4-6 months).
- **FHLMC**: Similar; some products actual/actual.
- **GNMA**: Full P&I pass-through until buyout; **highest advance burden**.

## Recovery
- Advances recovered from proceeds at loan resolution (payoff, mod, liquidation).
- Time value of money = cost of carry (funding cost × months outstanding).

## Impact on Multiple
- Advance cost = **funding rate × avg advance balance × duration**.
- In high-rate environments, advance carrying cost balloons — hurts multiple significantly.
- Portfolios with elevated DQ or judicial-state concentration: much larger advance drag.

## Financing
- Servicers use MSR facilities, advance receivables facilities (AR notes) to fund advances.
