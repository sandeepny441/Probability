**Here's a clean summary table** for a **$400,000 loan** example (wholesale lender like UWM):

| Item                        | Value                  | Math / Explanation                                                                 | Impact on Lender |
|-----------------------------|------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------|
| **Loan Amount**            | $400,000              | Base loan size                                                                    | - |
| **Note Rate** (to Borrower)| 6.375%                | Final rate borrower pays (selected from Rate Sheet)                               | Higher rate creates room for margin |
| **Rate Sheet Price**       | ~99.50 to 100.00      | What broker sees (rate + points/credits)                                          | Lender's offered pricing |
| **Investor Sale Price**    | 102.25                | What lender sells the loan for on secondary market (premium from note rate)       | $409,000 received |
| **Lender Margin** (built-in) | 75–100 bps (0.75–1.00%) | Spread embedded in rate sheet between investor bid and broker-facing price        | Part of total gross spread |
| **Broker Compensation**    | 1.00% ($4,000)        | Lender-paid commission (YSP) funded by the margin/rate                            | Paid out at closing |
| **Origination + Other Costs** | 0.75% ($3,000)     | Processing, underwriting, hedging, etc.                                           | Paid out |
| **Gross Gain on Sale**     | 225 bps ($9,000)      | Investor sale price minus par funded (102.25 − 100.00)                            | Before broker pay & costs |
| **Net Profit (Gain-on-Sale Margin)** | **50 bps** ($2,000) | After all costs & broker pay — lender's real **profit** per loan                 | **Lender's bottom line** |

### Quick Math Walkthrough (for this example)
- Lender receives from investor: **$409,000** (102.25 price)
- Funds borrower: **$400,000** (par)
- **Gross gain on sale:** **$9,000** (225 bps)
- Pays broker: **−$4,000** (100 bps LPC)
- Pays other costs: **−$3,000** (75 bps)
- **Net Profit** = **$409,000 − $400,000 − $4,000 − $3,000 = $2,000** (50 bps)

**Why sale price is 102.25, not 101.00:** The note rate (6.375%) sits above that day's par coupon, so the loan sells at a secondary-market premium. The rate sheet (~99.50–100.00) is broker-facing pricing after UWM embeds margin and the broker selects 100 bps LPC. Gross spread must cover broker comp + costs + profit — at 101.00 (only 100 bps gross), a 100 bps broker payout and 75 bps costs would leave the lender underwater.

**Key Relationship**:
- **Note Rate** ← chosen from **Rate Sheet**
- **Rate Sheet** = Investor pricing + **Lender Margin**
- **Lender Margin** covers broker pay + costs + **Profit**
- **Profit** = Actual realized gain-on-sale (what’s left after everything)

This structure is how wholesale lenders balance competitive rates for borrowers while staying profitable.
