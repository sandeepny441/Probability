# Mortgage-Backed Securities (MBS) & Fixed Income Jargon Guide

A comprehensive reference for understanding common terminology used in mortgage-backed securities, pooling, and bond markets.

## Core Terms (A-Z)

| Term                                                | Definition                                                                                                                            |
| --------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Accrued Interest**                                | Interest that has been earned but not yet paid to the bondholder; calculated between coupon payment dates                             |
| **Agency MBS**                                      | Mortgage-backed securities issued or guaranteed by government-sponsored enterprises (Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac, Ginnie Mae)             |
| **Amortization**                                    | The process of paying down a loan over time through regular principal and interest payments                                           |
| **Amortization Schedule**                           | A table showing each payment on a loan, breaking down how much goes to principal vs. interest                                         |
| **ARM**                                             | Adjustable Rate Mortgage; a mortgage where the interest rate changes periodically based on market conditions                          |
| **Ask Price**                                       | The price at which a seller is willing to sell a security (opposite of bid price)                                                     |
| **Asset-Backed Securities (ABS)**                   | Securities backed by a pool of assets (like auto loans or credit card receivables) instead of mortgages                               |
| **Average Life**                                    | The weighted average time it takes to receive principal repayment on a bond; accounts for prepayments                                 |
| **Basis Points**                                    | A unit of measure for interest rates; 1 basis point = 0.01% (100 basis points = 1%)                                                   |
| **Bid Price**                                       | The price at which a buyer is willing to buy a security (opposite of ask price)                                                       |
| **Bid-Ask Spread**                                  | The difference between what buyers will pay and what sellers will accept; indicates market liquidity                                  |
| **Bond Rating**                                     | A credit quality assessment (AAA, AA, A, BBB, etc.) indicating the likelihood a bond issuer will repay debt                           |
| **Book Value**                                      | The value of a mortgage pool recorded on a servicer's or investor's books                                                             |
| **Bullet Bond**                                     | A bond where the entire principal is repaid in one lump sum at maturity, not gradually                                                |
| **Call Option**                                     | The right (but not obligation) for an issuer to repay a bond before its maturity date                                                 |
| **Callable Bond**                                   | A bond that can be redeemed by the issuer before the stated maturity date                                                             |
| **Cash Flow**                                       | The movement of money in and out; for MBS, includes principal and interest payments                                                   |
| **CMO**                                             | Collateralized Mortgage Obligation; a structured security that divides mortgage cash flows into different priority levels (tranches)  |
| **Collateral**                                      | Assets pledged as security for a loan; in MBS, the underlying mortgages are the collateral                                            |
| **Collateralized Debt Obligation (CDO)**            | A security backed by a pool of debt obligations, often including mortgage-backed securities                                           |
| **Constant Prepayment Rate (CPR)**                  | An annualized estimate of how fast mortgages in a pool will be paid off early                                                         |
| **Coupon**                                          | The periodic interest payment made to a bondholder; expressed as a percentage of face value                                           |
| **Coupon Rate**                                     | The annual interest rate paid on a bond (e.g., 4% coupon means 4% annual interest)                                                    |
| **Credit Risk**                                     | The risk that a borrower will default and fail to make required payments                                                              |
| **Credit Spread**                                   | The difference in yield between a risky bond and a risk-free bond (like U.S. Treasuries)                                              |
| **Custodian**                                       | A third party that holds and safeguards the mortgages or securities in a pool                                                         |
| **Default**                                         | Failure to make required loan or bond payments on time                                                                                |
| **Delinquency**                                     | A situation where a borrower is late on payments but has not yet defaulted                                                            |
| **Discount**                                        | When a bond's market price is below its face value (occurs when rates have risen since issuance)                                      |
| **Discount Bond**                                   | A bond trading below par value; typically occurs when its coupon is lower than current market rates                                   |
| **Discount Rate**                                   | The interest rate used to calculate the present value of future cash flows                                                            |
| **Dollar Roll**                                     | A short-term trading transaction where an investor sells an MBS and simultaneously agrees to repurchase a similar one at a later date |
| **Duration**                                        | A measure of how sensitive a bond's price is to changes in interest rates; expressed in years                                         |
| **Effective Duration**                              | A measure of duration that accounts for the impact of prepayments and callable features                                               |
| **Extension Risk**                                  | The risk that falling interest rates will cause prepayments to slow, extending the bond's life longer than expected                   |
| **Face Value**                                      | The principal amount of a bond that will be repaid at maturity (also called par value or par)                                         |
| **Fannie Mae**                                      | Federal National Mortgage Association; a government-sponsored enterprise that buys, guarantees, and securitizes mortgages             |
| **Fixed-Rate Mortgage**                             | A mortgage where the interest rate stays the same throughout the loan term                                                            |
| **Floater**                                         | A bond with a variable coupon rate that adjusts periodically based on a reference rate                                                |
| **Floater Cap**                                     | The maximum interest rate a floating-rate bond can pay                                                                                |
| **Floater Floor**                                   | The minimum interest rate a floating-rate bond can pay                                                                                |
| **Forward MBS**                                     | An MBS that will be delivered in the future, not immediately                                                                          |
| **Freddie Mac**                                     | Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corporation; a government-sponsored enterprise that buys and securitizes mortgages                         |
| **Freddie Mac PC**                                  | Participation Certificate issued by Freddie Mac; an agency MBS product                                                                |
| **Ginnie Mae**                                      | Government National Mortgage Association; issues MBS backed by government-insured loans (FHA, VA, USDA)                               |
| **Gross Coupon**                                    | The total interest rate on mortgages in a pool before the servicer takes their cut                                                    |
| **GSE**                                             | Government-Sponsored Enterprise; includes Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac, and Ginnie Mae                                                     |
| **Hedge**                                           | A transaction designed to reduce or offset the risk of another investment                                                             |
| **Index**                                           | A benchmark rate (like SOFR or the 10-year Treasury yield) used to determine interest rates on adjustable products                    |
| **Interest Rate Risk**                              | The risk that bond prices will fall if interest rates rise                                                                            |
| **Interest-Only Strip (IO)**                        | A security that receives only the interest payments from a mortgage pool, not the principal                                           |
| **Inverse Floater**                                 | A floating-rate bond where the coupon moves in the opposite direction of market rates                                                 |
| **Liquidity**                                       | The ease with which a security can be bought or sold quickly without significantly affecting its price                                |
| **Liquidity Risk**                                  | The risk that a security cannot be sold quickly without taking a significant loss                                                     |
| **LTV**                                             | Loan-to-Value; the loan amount divided by the property's value, expressed as a percentage                                             |
| **FICO Score**                                      | A credit score ranging from 300 to 850 that measures an individual's creditworthiness                                                 |
| **Make-Whole Provision**                            | A clause allowing a borrower to pay off a loan early without penalty, usually by compensating the lender for lost interest            |
| **Maturity Date**                                   | The date on which the principal of a bond must be repaid                                                                              |
| **Moral Hazard**                                    | The risk that one party will take excessive risks knowing another party bears the cost of failure                                     |
| **Mortgage Pool**                                   | A collection of mortgages bundled together to create mortgage-backed securities                                                       |
| **Mortgage Pass-Through**                           | An MBS where investors receive a proportional share of all principal and interest payments (minus servicing fees)                     |
| **Negative Convexity**                              | A characteristic of callable bonds where price appreciation is limited when rates fall due to call option                             |
| **Non-Agency MBS**                                  | Mortgage-backed securities not issued or guaranteed by government-sponsored enterprises; subject to credit risk                       |
| **OAS**                                             | Option-Adjusted Spread; a measure of yield spread that accounts for embedded options like prepayment                                  |
| **Overcollateralization**                           | When the value of collateral exceeds the amount of debt issued against it; provides a cushion for bondholders                         |
| **Par Value**                                       | The face value of a bond; the amount that will be repaid at maturity                                                                  |
| **Pass-Through Rate**                               | The interest rate paid to MBS investors; typically less than the gross coupon due to servicing fees                                   |
| **Payment Delay**                                   | The number of days between when a mortgage payment is collected and when it's passed through to MBS investors                         |
| **Pool Factor**                                     | A number showing what percentage of the original principal remains outstanding (ranges from 1.0 to 0.0)                               |
| **Prepayment**                                      | Early repayment of a loan before the scheduled maturity date                                                                          |
| **Prepayment Penalty**                              | A fee charged to a borrower for paying off a loan early                                                                               |
| **Prepayment Risk**                                 | The risk that rising interest rates will slow prepayments, extending the bond's duration                                              |
| **Premium Bond**                                    | A bond trading above par value; typically occurs when its coupon is higher than current market rates                                  |
| **Principal**                                       | The original amount borrowed or the face value of a bond                                                                              |
| **Principal-Only Strip (PO)**                       | A security that receives only the principal payments from a mortgage pool, not the interest                                           |
| **Prospectus**                                      | A detailed document describing a security's terms, risks, and the issuer                                                              |
| **PSA Model**                                       | A standard model that predicts mortgage prepayment speeds; 100% PSA is the baseline assumption                                        |
| **Purchase Agreement**                              | A contract between the mortgage originator and the entity buying the mortgages for securitization                                     |
| **Qualified Mortgage (QM)**                         | A mortgage meeting specific criteria established by regulations; offers certain legal protections                                     |
| **Ramp-Up**                                         | The initial period of a CMO tranche where it doesn't receive principal payments yet                                                   |
| **Real Estate Mortgage Investment Conduit (REMIC)** | A special legal structure used for securitizing mortgages with tax benefits                                                           |
| **Refinancing**                                     | The process of replacing an existing loan with a new loan, typically to get better terms                                              |
| **Reinvestment Risk**                               | The risk that interest and principal payments will have to be reinvested at lower rates                                               |
| **Remainder Tranche**                               | The lowest priority tranche in a CMO that absorbs losses first                                                                        |
| **Repurchase Agreement (Repo)**                     | A short-term transaction where securities are sold with an agreement to repurchase them at a set price                                |
| **Residual Tranche**                                | The final CMO tranche that receives whatever cash remains after other tranches are paid                                               |
| **Reverse Floater**                                 | Same as inverse floater; a bond with a coupon that moves opposite to interest rate changes                                            |
| **Reverse Mortgage**                                | A mortgage allowing homeowners to borrow against their home equity, with repayment typically due upon death or sale                   |
| **S&P 500**                                         | The Standard & Poor's 500 Index; a benchmark index of large-cap U.S. stocks                                                           |
| **Secondary Market**                                | The market where existing securities are bought and sold between investors                                                            |
| **Securitization**                                  | The process of pooling loans and creating tradable securities backed by those loans                                                   |
| **Servicer**                                        | The company responsible for collecting mortgage payments, maintaining escrow accounts, and managing delinquencies                     |
| **Servicing Fee**                                   | The fee charged by a servicer, typically 0.25% to 0.50% of the outstanding balance annually                                           |
| **Settlement Date**                                 | The date when payment is delivered and ownership of a security transfers from seller to buyer                                         |
| **Shelf Registration**                              | A process allowing an issuer to register securities that can be sold over time without additional registration                        |
| **SOFR**                                            | Secured Overnight Financing Rate; a benchmark interest rate replacing LIBOR                                                           |
| **Spread**                                          | The difference in yield between two securities; indicates relative value and risk                                                     |
| **Stated Maturity**                                 | The date the final payment on a bond is due                                                                                           |
| **Step-Up Bond**                                    | A bond where the coupon rate increases at predetermined intervals                                                                     |
| **Stripped MBS**                                    | An MBS that has been divided into separate principal-only (PO) and interest-only (IO) securities                                      |
| **Subordination**                                   | A structure where some securities have lower priority claims than others in receiving cash flows                                      |
| **Support Tranche**                                 | In a CMO, a tranche that provides support to other tranches by absorbing prepayment risk                                              |
| **Swap**                                            | A transaction where two parties exchange cash flows, often used to hedge interest rate risk                                           |
| **TAC Bond**                                        | Targeted Amortization Class; a CMO tranche designed to have more stable prepayment characteristics                                    |
| **Tail Risk**                                       | The risk of rare, extreme events that have a small probability but large impact                                                       |
| **Tenor**                                           | The time remaining until a bond's maturity                                                                                            |
| **Term Structure**                                  | The relationship between bond yields and their time to maturity; often shown as a yield curve                                         |
| **Trade Date**                                      | The date when a securities transaction is agreed upon                                                                                 |
| **Tranche**                                         | A portion or layer of a CMO or other structured security with its own priority, coupon, and maturity                                  |
| **Treasury Bond**                                   | A long-term debt security issued by the U.S. government; considered risk-free                                                         |
| **Underwriter**                                     | A firm that buys securities from an issuer and sells them to investors                                                                |
| **Underwriting Standards**                          | The criteria used to determine whether a borrower qualifies for a mortgage                                                            |
| **WAC**                                             | Weighted Average Coupon; the average interest rate on mortgages in a pool, weighted by balance                                        |
| **WAM**                                             | Weighted Average Maturity; the average remaining time to maturity of mortgages in a pool, weighted by balance                         |
| **Yield**                                           | The annual return on a bond investment, expressed as a percentage                                                                     |
| **Yield Curve**                                     | A graph showing bond yields at different maturities; shape indicates economic expectations                                            |
| **Yield Spread**                                    | The difference in yield between two bonds, often indicating relative risk                                                             |
| **Z-Tranche**                                       | An accrual bond in a CMO that receives no cash payments until earlier tranches are retired; interest accrues                          |

## Key Concepts Summary

### Risk Types in MBS
- **Prepayment Risk**: Rates fall → borrowers refinance → securities pay off early
- **Extension Risk**: Rates rise → borrowers hold mortgages → securities mature later than expected
- **Interest Rate Risk**: Rate changes affect bond prices inversely
- **Credit Risk**: Borrower default (less relevant for agency MBS)

### MBS Structure Layers
1. **Mortgages** → pooled together
2. **MBS/Pass-through** → investors receive principal + interest
3. **CMO** → mortgages split into tranches with different priority levels
4. **Stripped MBS** → separated into principal-only (PO) and interest-only (IO) pieces

### Payment Waterfall (Typical CMO)
Interest flows → Senior tranches → Subordinated tranches
Principal flows → Sequential tranches → PAC/TAC tranches → Support tranches → Residual
