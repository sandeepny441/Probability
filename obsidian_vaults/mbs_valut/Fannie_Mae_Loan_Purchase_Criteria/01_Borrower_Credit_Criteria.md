# Borrower Credit Criteria

Fannie Mae evaluates borrower creditworthiness before purchasing a loan.

## Key Requirements
- **Minimum FICO**: Generally 620 for most conforming loans; 640+ for manual underwriting; HomeReady may allow 620.
- **Credit Score Source**: Classic FICO from all three bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion). Middle score used; lowest middle if multiple borrowers.
- **Tradelines**: Sufficient credit depth — typically 3 tradelines with 12+ months history, or non-traditional credit documentation.
- **Derogatory Events** (waiting periods):
  - Bankruptcy Ch. 7: 4 years (2 with extenuating circumstances)
  - Bankruptcy Ch. 13: 2 years from discharge
  - Foreclosure: 7 years (3 with extenuating circumstances)
  - Short sale / deed-in-lieu: 4 years
- **Recent Delinquencies**: No 60+ day mortgage lates in last 12 months.
- **Collections/Judgments**: Must be evaluated; large unpaid collections may need to be paid off.
