# Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI)

DTI measures borrower's ability to repay by comparing monthly debt to gross monthly income.

## Ratios
- **Front-end (Housing DTI)**: PITI ÷ gross monthly income. No hard cap, but monitored.
- **Back-end (Total DTI)**: (PITI + all other monthly debts) ÷ gross monthly income.

## Limits
- **Maximum DTI**: 45% standard; up to **50%** allowed via DU with strong compensating factors (reserves, high credit, low LTV).
- **Manual underwriting**: Typically capped at 36% (45% with compensating factors).
- **HomeReady**: Same 50% max via DU.

## Debts Counted
- Installment loans with >10 payments remaining
- Revolving debt (minimum payments)
- Alimony/child support
- Student loans (IBR or 1% of balance if deferred)
- Co-signed debts (unless 12 months paid by others)
