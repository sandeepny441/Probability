# Loan Size

## EPO Risk by Balance
- **Small balance (< $150k)**: **Low EPO risk**. Closing costs of refi ($3–5k) eat too much of the potential savings; borrower rarely refis.
- **Mid balance ($200–450k)**: Baseline EPO behavior.
- **High balance / jumbo ($700k+)**: **Elevated EPO risk**. Every 25 bp rate change yields large dollar savings, easily justifying refi costs. Borrowers also more financially sophisticated.

## Investor View
- High-balance loans need extra premium haircut or overlay.
- Small-balance loans command a premium in MSR / whole loan pricing due to prepay stickiness.

## Math
- On a $700k loan, a 50 bp rate drop = ~$3,500/yr savings → refi pays for itself in ~1 year.
- On a $120k loan, same 50 bp drop = ~$600/yr → 5+ year break-even → rarely refis.
