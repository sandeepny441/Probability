# Lender Pricing Strategy

## Above-Market Pricing → EPO
- Lenders consistently pricing 25-50 bps above the market rate on similar loans → their book is systematically high-SATO → EPO-prone.
- Even if borrower isn't sophisticated at origination, competitors call within weeks with better offers.

## Below-Market ("Rate Leader") Pricing → Adverse Selection of EPO
- Rate-leader positioning attracts **rate-shoppers**, who by definition will shop again when rates change → high EPO.
- Paradoxical: aggressive low pricing brings in the segment most likely to leave.

## Point Structures
- Lenders selling loans with heavy **lender credits / negative points** ship structurally EPO-prone loans.
- Loans with borrower-paid points show lower EPO.

## Rate Sheet Behaviors
- Frequent intraday rate sheet updates → LO rate-timing games → EPO.
- Sticky rate sheets → healthier book.

## Buydowns and Promotional Rates
- Temporary buydowns (2-1, 3-2-1) are premised on refi in year 2-3 → structurally EPO products.
- "Refi for free later" promotions → engineered EPO.
