# Points Paid at Origination (Discount Points)

## Signal Interpretation
- **Borrower paid 0 discount points**: Weak rate commitment → willing to accept higher rate → less rate-sensitive OR expects to refi soon → moderate/high EPO risk.
- **Borrower paid significant discount points (1-3)**: **Lower EPO risk**. Borrower has "sunk cost" psychology — they paid up front for a lower rate over the loan's life and won't recoup that if they refi soon.
- **Lender/broker paid points (credit)**: Borrower took **higher rate + closing cost credit** — very common EPO setup. Lender essentially bribed borrower into accepting a rate they'd want to refi out of.

## Zero-Cost / Negative Point Loans
- Above-market rate with lender credit covering closing costs.
- Borrower has no sunk cost → EPO whenever slightly lower rate available.
- **Extremely common EPO pattern.**

## Investor Screening
- Investors look at points paid + net closing cost credit fields to flag likely EPO.
- Correspondent purchase agreements sometimes exclude no-point/lender-credit loans.
