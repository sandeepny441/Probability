# LO Compensation Structure

## Comp-Driven EPO Behavior
- **Basis points per loan (LO paid 100-175 bps of loan amount)**: LO incentivized to maximize volume, not retention.
- **Volume bonuses / tiered comp**: End-of-quarter push may lead to refi-of-own-book to hit tier.
- **Comp tied to margin / rate**: LO steers borrower to higher rate → high SATO → EPO.

## Regulatory Framework
- **LO Comp Rule (Reg Z 2011)** prohibits comp based on loan terms other than amount — but pricing games persist.

## "Churn" Compensation Games
- Some organizations pay full comp on every refi regardless of prior origination.
- Others impose **clawback windows** (e.g., no comp on refi within 6 months of prior origination by same LO).

## Lender-Level Compensation Practices
- Aggressive volume-based comp structures → higher EPO rate at lender level.
- Comp tied to retention / long-life loans → healthier books.
