# EPO Contract Terms

The contractual language in purchase agreements defining EPO events and remedies.

## Standard EPO Clause Structure
- **EPO Window**: Typically 3, 6, or 9 months from purchase date. Some go to 12 months.
- **Trigger Event**: Full payoff of the loan (refi, sale, or otherwise) within window.
- **Remedy**: Seller refunds all or a portion of the premium paid.

## Common Remedy Structures
- **Full premium refund** if EPO within Month 1-3.
- **Sliding scale**: 100% refund Month 1 → 50% by Month 6.
- **Fixed dollar penalty per loan** (some contracts).
- **Servicing-Released Premium (SRP) refund**: If MSR was included in purchase and loan pays off early.

## Early Payment Default (EPD) — Related But Different
- **EPD**: Borrower goes delinquent early (typically 60 DPD within first 3-6 payments).
- Triggers **repurchase** at par (much more damaging than EPO refund).
- Sometimes conflated colloquially; important to distinguish.

## GSE / Government EPO
- Fannie/Freddie have their own EPO expectations for seller/servicers.
- GNMA imposes seasoning requirements (6-month payment seasoning for VA IRRRL, FHA streamline).
- Excessive EPO can trigger GSE review of seller.

## Correspondent Master Purchase Agreements
- Include reps around origination quality, non-solicitation, and EPO/EPD language.
- Enforcement done through post-close audit and clawback demands.
