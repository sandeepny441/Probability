# Lender Marketing Practices

Lender's downstream marketing behavior drives ongoing EPO exposure to buyers.

## Trigger Lead Buying/Selling
- **Trigger leads**: When any lender pulls a borrower's credit for mortgage, credit bureaus (Experian, Equifax, TransUnion) sell that borrower's info to competing lenders **within 24 hours**.
- Buyers of trigger leads call borrowers within hours of a loan closing.
- Lenders whose brokers/LOs are known trigger-lead **buyers** are also often **sellers** through borrower databases.

## Post-Close Marketing
- Aggressive lenders re-market to their own recently-closed borrowers (refi solicitations, HELOC offers, cash-out pitches).
- If they've sold the loan, this creates EPO for the buyer.

## Database Practices
- Lender uses closed-loan database for continual re-marketing.
- Some sell borrower lists to affiliates or third parties.

## "Watch Lists" and Retention Offers
- Some lenders proactively watch **rate reductions** and email old borrowers.
- Great for lender's own book if retaining servicing → destructive for third-party MSR buyer.

## Non-Solicitation Clauses
- MSR purchase contracts and correspondent agreements typically include non-solicitation for 12-24 months.
- Enforcement is uneven; violation → damages / clawback.
