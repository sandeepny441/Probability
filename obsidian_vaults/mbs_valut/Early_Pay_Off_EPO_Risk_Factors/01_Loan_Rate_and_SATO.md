# Loan Rate and SATO (Spread at Origination)

**The single strongest EPO predictor.**

## SATO Definition
- **SATO** = Note rate on the loan **minus** the prevailing market rate for a comparable loan at time of origination.
- Positive SATO = borrower is paying **above market**.
- High positive SATO strongly indicates EPO risk.

## Why It Predicts EPO
- If borrower is 50-100+ bps above market, any competing lender who identifies them (trigger lead lists) can offer immediate refi savings.
- Recapture-hungry lenders and lead brokers actively call new-origination borrowers with high SATO.
- Borrower has clear economic incentive to refinance right away.

## Interpretation
- **SATO > +75 bps**: Very high EPO risk.
- **SATO +25 to +75 bps**: Moderate.
- **SATO near 0 or negative**: Low EPO risk.

## Common SATO Causes
- Borrower didn't shop (took first offer).
- Broker steering (higher commissions on higher-rate).
- Weak borrower profile priced up (but not always — see FICO context).
- Loan officer didn't disclose competitive pricing.

## Investor Screening
- Investors compare each loan's note rate to a **market rate benchmark** (typically FNMA/FHLMC par rate at rate lock date).
- Loans in the high-SATO tail are haircut or excluded.
