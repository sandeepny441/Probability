# Lender Business Model

Lender's business model shapes systemic EPO risk of their entire book.

## Lender Types
- **Depository Banks (Chase, Wells, BofA)**: Lowest EPO. Cross-sell, deposit relationship, portfolio retention on some loans.
- **Credit Unions**: Low EPO. Membership relationship, low churn culture.
- **Large IMBs (Independent Mortgage Banks) — Retail-focused (Rocket, LoanDepot, Guild)**: Moderate EPO. Strong recapture teams but also refi-boom driven.
- **Wholesale Lenders (UWM, HomePoint historically)**: EPO risk depends heavily on brokers, but wholesale channel structurally EPO-prone.
- **Correspondent Sellers**: Highest EPO — no relationship, transaction-driven.
- **Retail IMBs with small footprint / referral-based**: Lower EPO.
- **Fintech (Better, Sage)**: Elevated EPO — online-shopper customer base.

## Culture Signals
- Aggressive volume growth targets → EPO-prone.
- "Rate leader" positioning → adverse selection of shoppers.
- Company promotions of same-lender refi ("refi now for free!") → self-churn built in.

## Investor View
- Aggregators maintain lender-by-lender EPO scorecards.
- Underperforming lenders receive lower purchase prices, tighter EPO clauses, or removal from approved list.
