# Lender Recapture Behavior

Lender's willingness/aggressiveness to refi its own book affects EPO to third-party buyers.

## The Recapture Paradox
- If lender **sold the servicing** to a third party (aggregator, MSR investor), any refi by lender = **EPO event** damaging the buyer.
- If lender **retained servicing**, recapture is neutral or positive (they keep the borrower).

## Common Bad Patterns
- Lender sells loan + MSR to aggregator, then refis the borrower 2 months later.
- This is **contractually addressed** by EPO clawback clauses — but the reputational/business relationship damage is real.

## Correspondent EPO Enforcement
- Aggregators enforce EPO clauses: premium refunded if loan pays off within window.
- Repeated violations → correspondent gets suspended or de-signed.

## "Voluntary Retention" Programs
- Some lenders promise buyer: "we will not refi loans we sold you for X months."
- Aggregators favor these sellers → better pricing.

## Servicer View
- Third-party MSR buyer wants **contractual non-solicitation** clause preventing seller from marketing to serviced borrowers.
