# Borrower Geography

## State/MSA Effects on EPO
- **High HPA states** (CA, WA, FL, TX in appreciation cycles): Rising equity → cash-out refi opportunity → elevated EPO.
- **Competitive lending markets** (dense metros with many broker/direct lenders): More trigger lead activity → more EPO.
- **Rural / small metro**: Fewer competing lenders reach the borrower → less EPO.

## State Regulatory Effects
- Some states have consumer-protection rules limiting rate refis within short window (rare).
- State-level trigger lead laws (a few states restrict trigger leads — MN, TX) reduce EPO in-state.

## Job-Market Volatility
- Tech-heavy metros (Bay Area, Austin, Seattle) → high income mobility → more moves/refis.
- Military-base areas (Norfolk, San Diego, Fayetteville) → PCS turnover.

## Natural Disaster Zones
- Fire/hurricane/flood zones: forced payoffs from insurance settlements, forbearance patterns can accelerate resolution.
