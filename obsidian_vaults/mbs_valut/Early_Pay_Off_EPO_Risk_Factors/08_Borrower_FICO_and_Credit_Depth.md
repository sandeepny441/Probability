# Borrower FICO and Credit Depth

## FICO & EPO
- **High FICO (760+)**: **Higher EPO risk** — sophisticated, rate-aware, easily qualifies for competing refis, actively shops.
- **Mid FICO (680–740)**: Baseline behavior.
- **Low FICO (< 660)**: Lower EPO risk short-term — few lenders will refi them at improved terms; competitive lead activity ignores them.

## Credit Depth & Tradelines
- Borrowers with **deep, high-limit credit** (multiple mortgages history, prime cards) more likely to have refi outreach and act on it.
- Thin-file borrowers less targeted by trigger leads.

## Credit Score Trajectory
- Borrower whose FICO **improved substantially between application and origination** — often EPO candidate. They may have been "pushed" into a non-optimal rate; once seasoning improves, they refi.
- Non-QM → conforming migration path uses this pattern.

## Investor Screening
- Investors sometimes overlay: "no premium bid on 780+ FICO with high SATO" — combination is EPO gold flag.
