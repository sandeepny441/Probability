# Borrower Income and DTI

## Signal
- **High income, low DTI**: Financially flexible, can absorb refi costs, more likely to shop, elevated EPO risk.
- **Tight DTI (45-50%)**: Refi qualifying is harder → lower EPO risk short-term, though refi can loosen DTI.

## Self-Employed Borrowers
- May originate on non-QM (bank statement, P&L) at higher rate → refi to conforming once tax returns are strong → **classic EPO pattern**.

## Rising Income
- W-2 borrowers who just got a raise / bonus / promotion may consider cash-out or rate refi → moderate EPO uplift.

## DTI Just Above Threshold (e.g., 43.1%)
- Borrower likely required manual UW or non-QM; will refi to QM once one debt is paid off.
