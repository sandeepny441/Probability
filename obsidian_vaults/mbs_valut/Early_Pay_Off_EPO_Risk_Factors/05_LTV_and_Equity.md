# LTV and Equity Position

## LTV Effect on EPO
- **Low LTV (< 70%)**: **Higher EPO risk** — borrower can refi easily, no MI required, appraisal issues rare, cash-out capacity.
- **Mid LTV (70–90%)**: Baseline behavior.
- **High LTV (> 95%)**: **Lower EPO risk near-term** — refi requires new MI, may not qualify without significant HPA gain.

## HPI-Adjusted (Current LTV)
- If HPI has risen since origination → borrower's effective equity has grown → refi capacity increased → EPO risk increased.
- Rising markets (post-origination) elevate EPO risk especially on high-original-LTV loans that quickly become low-current-LTV.

## Equity Withdrawal Loans
- Cash-out refis leave borrower with lower equity but proven cash-out behavior → they may cash out again if HPA continues.
