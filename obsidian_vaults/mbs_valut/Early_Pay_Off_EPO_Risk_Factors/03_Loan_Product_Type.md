# Loan Product Type

Product structure influences EPO propensity.

## Products & EPO Risk
- **30-yr Fixed**: Baseline EPO behavior.
- **15-yr Fixed**: Somewhat lower EPO — borrower committed to aggressive paydown, more relationship-oriented.
- **ARMs (5/6, 7/6, 10/6)**: EPO less common early — borrower took ARM for a reason (short-term horizon or teaser rate).
- **Temporary Buydown Loans (2-1, 3-2-1)**: Elevated EPO — borrower banking on refi before permanent rate kicks in.
- **FHA / VA**: **Very high EPO risk** — streamline refi programs (FHA Streamline, VA IRRRL) make refi extremely easy with minimal documentation. Trigger lead activity in this segment is intense.
- **Non-QM / Bank Statement / DSCR**: High EPO — borrowers often refi into conforming as they season into stronger credit profile.
- **Jumbo**: Varies; sophisticated borrowers who shop aggressively.

## Government Loan Distinction
- **VA IRRRL**: No appraisal, no VOI, minimal doc → EPO within 6 months common.
- **FHA Streamline**: Similar mechanics; GNMA imposed **6-month payment seasoning** requirement in part because of EPO abuse.
