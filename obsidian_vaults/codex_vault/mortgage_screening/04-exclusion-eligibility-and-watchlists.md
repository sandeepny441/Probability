# 04. Exclusion, Eligibility, and Watchlists

## Why These Lists Exist

Not every screening list is about sanctions or fraud.

Some lists ask:

```text
Is this party allowed to participate in this program?
```

Others ask:

```text
Has this person or company been suspended, debarred, excluded, or made ineligible?
```

These can affect whether a loan can be originated, insured, guaranteed, sold, or serviced.

## HUD Limited Denial of Participation

HUD's Limited Denial of Participation, or LDP, can restrict a person or company from participating in HUD programs.

Mortgage relevance:

```text
FHA loans
HUD program participation
Builders, sellers, lenders, brokers, appraisers, consultants, or other parties depending on the case
```

Common operational question:

```text
Is any relevant transaction party on the HUD LDP list?
```

If yes, the loan may be ineligible or require removal/replacement/escalation depending on the party and program.

## SAM Exclusions

SAM.gov includes federal exclusion records, including parties that are debarred, suspended, or otherwise excluded from certain federal transactions.

Mortgage relevance:

```text
Government loan programs
Federal program participation
Agency and investor eligibility checks
Vendor and counterparty controls
```

Common operational question:

```text
Is the borrower, seller, broker, appraiser, contractor, company, or vendor excluded from federal programs?
```

## CAIVRS

CAIVRS stands for Credit Alert Verification Reporting System.

It is used for certain government-backed loan programs to identify borrowers with delinquent federal debt or prior claim/default issues.

Mortgage relevance:

```text
FHA
VA
USDA
Other federal credit programs depending on access/rule
```

Common operational question:

```text
Does this borrower have a federal debt/default issue that affects program eligibility?
```

Important distinction:

```text
CAIVRS is not an all-purpose fraud list.
It is a federal credit eligibility control.
```

## FHFA Suspended Counterparty Program

FHFA maintains a Suspended Counterparty Program for individuals and entities suspended from doing business with regulated entities after certain findings.

Mortgage relevance:

```text
Fannie Mae / Freddie Mac ecosystem
Federal Home Loan Bank ecosystem
Counterparty and participant eligibility
```

Common operational question:

```text
Is a transaction party or business counterparty suspended by FHFA?
```

## Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac Exclusionary Lists

The GSEs maintain or reference exclusionary and ineligible-party controls.

Mortgage relevance:

```text
Loan delivery eligibility
Seller / servicer requirements
Parties to the transaction
Participants with fraud, misrepresentation, or unacceptable conduct history
```

Common operational question:

```text
Can this loan be sold to the investor with these parties involved?
```

These lists may not be fully public in the same way OFAC or SAM is. Approved sellers/servicers often access requirements through investor systems and guides.

## NMLS and Licensing Checks

NMLS Consumer Access helps verify mortgage loan originator and company licensing information.

Mortgage relevance:

```text
Is the MLO licensed?
Is the company active?
Is the license valid for the state?
Are there regulatory actions or status issues?
```

This is not a borrower bad-actor list.

It is a licensing and authority-to-originate check.

## Internal Watchlists

Lenders often maintain internal controls such as:

```text
Do-not-use broker list
Do-not-use appraiser list
Suspended seller list
High-risk settlement agent list
Prior confirmed fraud participant list
Repurchase / indemnification issue list
Early payment default pattern list
Vendor restriction list
```

Internal lists are powerful but sensitive.

Good practice:

```text
Use objective criteria.
Control who can add names.
Document why a party is listed.
Review periodically.
Allow correction when appropriate.
Avoid unsupported gossip.
Watch fair lending and defamation risk.
```

## PEP and Adverse Media

Some AML programs also use:

```text
Politically exposed person screening
Negative news / adverse media screening
High-risk geography screening
High-risk occupation or business screening
```

These are usually risk indicators, not automatic disqualifiers.

They help decide whether enhanced due diligence or escalation is needed.

## The Most Important Idea

Each list answers a different question.

```text
OFAC:
  Are we legally restricted from transacting?

HUD LDP / SAM / FHFA / GSE exclusion:
  Is this party excluded or ineligible?

CAIVRS:
  Is the borrower eligible for this government credit program?

NMLS:
  Is the originator/company licensed and active?

FraudGuard-style report:
  Are there risk signals that need verification?

Internal watchlist:
  Has our own institution decided this party needs restriction or escalation?
```
