# 05. When to Screen

## The Simple Timeline

Think of screening as a series of gates.

```text
Broker / vendor onboarding
Application
Processing
Underwriting
Clear-to-close
Funding
Post-closing
Investor delivery
Servicing
Transfer / payoff / liquidation
```

Each gate answers a different version of:

```text
Are we allowed to keep going?
```

## Timing Matrix

| Stage | What to Screen | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Broker / vendor onboarding | NMLS, licensing, sanctions, SAM, internal watchlists, approval status | Do not open the channel to a restricted or unapproved party |
| Application / file setup | Borrower, co-borrower, entity borrower, guarantor, broker, MLO | Catch sanctions, identity, fraud, and eligibility issues early |
| Processing | Added parties, updated addresses, title parties, seller, POA, trust, employer concerns | New facts can create new risks |
| Underwriting | Fraud report alerts, CAIVRS when applicable, program eligibility, investor exclusion checks | Do not approve a loan with unresolved fraud or eligibility problems |
| Clear-to-close | OFAC refresh, exclusion refresh, unresolved fraud conditions | Verify the file is clean before closing documents and funding |
| Funding | Final sanctions / party controls, wire recipient controls, settlement agent controls | Prevent prohibited or misdirected funds |
| Post-closing QC | Re-verify income, occupancy, identity, appraisal, title, fraud alerts | Catch defects before delivery or early in the life of the loan |
| Investor delivery | GSE/investor-specific exclusion, sanctions, eligibility, fraud reporting status | Avoid delivering an ineligible loan |
| Servicing | OFAC monitoring, payment restrictions, borrower changes, suspicious activity, deceased borrower, fraud findings | Restrictions can arise after closing |
| Transfer / payoff / liquidation | Counterparty, payoff recipient, wire, foreclosure/bankruptcy parties | End-of-life events still carry sanctions and fraud risk |

## Before Granting the Loan

Before closing/funding, the lender is trying to avoid:

```text
Funding a loan to a sanctioned party
Using an excluded appraiser, broker, seller, or vendor
Approving a borrower ineligible for a government program
Missing identity theft
Missing income or employment fabrication
Missing occupancy fraud
Missing property flip or valuation fraud
Missing undisclosed relationships
```

Pre-funding controls are the strongest because the lender can still stop the loan before money leaves.

## After Granting the Loan

After closing, the lender may still need to screen or monitor because:

```text
A party can be added to a sanctions list later.
Fraud may be discovered in post-closing QC.
An investor may identify a defect.
The borrower may default early.
Servicing events may reveal identity, occupancy, or payment issues.
The loan may not yet have been sold or delivered.
The lender may have repurchase or indemnification exposure.
```

Post-closing screening does not undo the funding, but it helps determine:

```text
Can we sell the loan?
Do we need to report something?
Do we need to block/reject a payment or transaction?
Do we need to cure, indemnify, or repurchase?
Do we need to restrict a broker, vendor, or other party going forward?
```

## Why Run the Same Name More Than Once?

Because the world changes.

```text
Sanctions lists update.
Transaction parties change.
A borrower may add a POA.
A seller may change title details.
A new settlement agent may enter the file.
The loan may sit in pipeline for weeks.
Fraud may emerge after closing.
```

One clean screen at application does not guarantee a clean file at funding.

## Practical Minimum Control Points

A practical lender workflow often has at least these controls:

```text
1. Screen at application.
2. Screen when a material new party is added.
3. Refresh before clear-to-close or funding.
4. Recheck before investor delivery when required.
5. Monitor servicing portfolio for sanctions and fraud events.
6. Keep audit evidence of screening and hit resolution.
```

## Important Evidence to Keep

For each screen, keep:

```text
Date and time
List or vendor source
List version or update date when available
Person/entity searched
Identifiers used
Result
Reviewer
Disposition
Supporting documents
Escalation notes
Final clearance or action
```

The audit trail matters almost as much as the screen itself.
