# 03. Buyback Process End-to-End (Operational Timeline)

Understanding the **process** is as important as the math, because every step has costs, evidence requirements, and opportunities to cure, rebut, or mitigate before full repurchase.

From UWM's perspective (high-volume wholesale seller to GSEs), the process is highly structured with strict timelines.

## High-Level Timeline (Typical GSE-Initiated Demand)

1. **Trigger Event** (ongoing)
   - GSE QC audit flags a defect.
   - EPD review identifies early delinquency + underwriting concern.
   - Fraud or servicing issue surfaces.
   - (Often 6–36+ months after sale, depending on defect type.)

2. **Demand / Notification** (Day 0)
   - GSE issues formal repurchase demand letter (or "make-whole" request).
   - Includes: Loan number, alleged defect(s), supporting documentation or findings summary, deadline for response (commonly 30–60 days to cure/rebut or indicate intent to repurchase).
   - UWM logs the demand in its QC/compliance system immediately.

3. **Initial Review & Triage** (Days 0–10)
   - UWM's QC, compliance, or legal team reviews the demand against the original file, underwriting notes, and any FraudGuard or screening history.
   - Classify: Curable defect? Rebuttable? Fraud-related? Broker-attributable?
   - Determine if additional information from broker or borrower is needed.
   - Assess recourse rights against the originating broker.

4. **Cure or Rebuttal Period** (Typically 30–90 days total from demand)
   - **Cure**: Provide missing documentation, corrected data, or evidence that resolves the defect (e.g., independent employer verification that was previously weak, occupancy evidence, appraisal support).
   - **Rebuttal**: Submit written response with evidence showing the GSE's finding is incorrect or the defect is not material / does not breach reps & warrants.
   - Many demands are resolved here without repurchase — this is a key win for UWM's operational teams.
   - If broker recourse applies, UWM simultaneously engages the broker for cure support or indemnification commitment.

5. **Escalation / Negotiation** (if not resolved)
   - Senior QC or legal escalation with GSE.
   - Possible offer of **indemnification** (lender agrees to cover future losses instead of immediate repurchase) or **fee in lieu** (pay a negotiated fee for release of the demand, available in some programs for performing loans with curable defects).
   - GSE may accept, counter, or insist on repurchase.

6. **Repurchase / Make-Whole** (if demand stands)
   - UWM wires the Repurchase Price (see math file) on the specified settlement date.
   - Loan (and servicing rights, if applicable) transfers back to UWM.
   - UWM updates its systems, notifies warehouse lender if the loan was still financed, and begins post-repurchase management (next file).

7. **Post-Resolution**
   - If cured/rebutted successfully: Demand closed, no loss booked beyond response costs.
   - If repurchased: Book the loan, establish reserves, pursue broker recourse and/or insurance claim, decide on disposition strategy.
   - Audit trail and reporting to investors/ regulators as required.
   - Update internal watchlists or broker scorecards if patterns emerge.

## Key UWM Operational Realities

- **Volume pressure**: With massive monthly production, UWM has dedicated teams and systems for demand response. Response quality and speed matter — poor responses lead to more repurchases.
- **Evidence is everything**: The screening and hit-handling discipline from the companion folder directly helps here. If a FraudGuard alert was cleared with strong independent verification and documented rationale, that evidence package is gold during rebuttal.
- **Broker leverage**: Wholesale model allows UWM to push many demands back to the broker for cure or payment. Retail or correspondent channels have different dynamics.
- **Cost of process**: Even "successful" rebuttals cost staff time, legal fees, and opportunity cost. Prevention upstream is cheaper.

## Common Outcomes (Industry / UWM Context)

- Significant percentage of demands are cured or rebutted successfully (exact % varies by GSE, lender performance, defect type).
- Of those that proceed to repurchase, many involve EPD or clear fraud/misrep where cure is impossible.
- Indemnification or fee alternatives are sometimes preferable to full repurchase when the loan is still performing and the defect is technical/curable.

## Why Process Discipline Matters for the Math

Every day a demand is open costs money (staff, potential carrying if repurchase is likely). Fast, high-quality responses that leverage good upstream documentation (fraud tool clearances, QC notes) directly reduce the number of times UWM has to pay the full Repurchase Price calculated in the previous file.

Strong process + strong prevention = materially lower net loss severity.
