# 08. Sources and References

These are primary or authoritative sources for the concepts in this buybacks education folder. Content changes; always verify with current GSE Selling/Servicing Guides, internal policy, and counsel.

## GSE / Investor Repurchase Rules & Calculations

- Fannie Mae Selling Guide: Loan Repurchases and Make Whole Payments (A2-3.2-01 and related sections)
- Fannie Mae Servicing Guide: Fannie Mae-Initiated Repurchases, Indemnifications, Make-Whole Payment Requests
- Freddie Mac Single-Family Seller/Servicer Guide: Sections on Repurchase (e.g., 3602 series on repurchase price calculation)
- FHFA and GSE periodic reports on repurchase trends and defect categories (public data releases)

## Industry Studies & Data on Severity and Drivers

- Stratmor / Reggora studies on GSE repurchase drivers and average costs (income and appraisal defects leading)
- Milliman Mortgage Repurchase Index (lifetime repurchase rate estimates by vintage)
- MBA and other mortgage banking research on production economics, GOS, and repurchase impact

## Accounting & Risk Management

- ASC 860 (Transfers and Servicing) — accounting for repurchases and gain-on-sale treatment
- CECL (Current Expected Credit Loss) guidance for provisioning on repurchased loans and contingent exposures
- OCC Comptroller's Handbook: Mortgage Banking (guidance on repurchase tracking, reserves, and risk management)

## Representations & Warranties Insurance

- Policy forms and carrier materials from providers active in the mortgage space (terms vary; lender-specific)
- Industry discussions on R&W insurance attachment points, coinsurance, and fraud exclusions

## Wholesale Lender / UWM Context

- UWM / UWMC public filings (10-K, 10-Q, earnings calls) — risk factor disclosures regarding repurchase and indemnification requests, warehouse line implications, and operational risk
- Broker agreement templates and recourse provisions (proprietary but standard in wholesale channel)

## Related Screening & Fraud Prevention (Companion Folder)

- See the "Mortgage Screening Lists" folder (especially FraudGuard-style tools, hit handling, and when-to-screen) for the upstream prevention controls that directly reduce buyback frequency and severity.

## Note

This educational material is for understanding concepts from a UWM lender operational and financial perspective. It is not legal, accounting, or compliance advice. For production decisions, rely on current investor contracts, GSE guides, internal policies, and qualified professionals. Repurchase data and economics are highly sensitive to economic cycles, interest rate environment, and lender-specific performance.
