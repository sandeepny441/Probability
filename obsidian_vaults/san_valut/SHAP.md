### Short Answer:
**You do NOT need to learn full Game Theory before understanding SHAP.**

You only need **one specific idea** from game theory — the **fair division of credit** — and even that can be learned *while* studying SHAP.

### My Recommended Path (Best Balance)

Here’s the efficient order I would follow if I were in your place wanting to “see inside” SHAP deeply:

| Layer | What to Learn | Time Needed | Why It Matters |
|-------|---------------|-------------|----------------|
| 1     | **Marginal Contribution** + Basic Probability | 1–2 hours | The absolute core atom of Shapley |
| 2     | **Averages over all combinations / permutations** | 2–3 hours | This is the heart of the formula |
| 3     | The **4 Fairness Axioms** (Efficiency, Symmetry, Dummy, Linearity) | 1 hour | Why Shapley is mathematically “correct” |
| 4     | How to adapt this to Machine Learning (the value function `v(S)`) | 2 hours | The bridge from game theory to SHAP |
| 5     | SHAP in practice (TreeSHAP, KernelSHAP, interpretations) | Ongoing | Application |
