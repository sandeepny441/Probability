
# **Historical Refinance / Prepayment / EPO Numbers Across the Mortgage Industry**

The industry usually tracks these through:

- CPR (Conditional Prepayment Rate)
- SMM (Single Monthly Mortality)
- Refinance share %
- Speeds by coupon/vintage
- Early Payoff / Early Payoff Default (EPO/EPD)
- PSA model speeds

The term “EPO” is used more internally by lenders/servicers, while capital markets investors usually discuss:

- prepayment speeds
- CPR
- refinance burnout
- turnover rates
- recapture rates.

---

# **1. Historical Refinance Waves (Big Picture)**

|**Period**|**Market Environment**|**Typical Industry Behavior**|
|---|---|---|
|2000–2003|Falling rates|Massive refinance boom|
|2004–2006|Rising rates|Prepays slowed|
|2008–2012|Financial crisis + QE|HARP refinance waves|
|2020–2021|COVID + ultra-low rates|One of largest refi booms ever|
|2022–2023|Rapid Fed hikes|Refi market collapsed|
|2024–2025|Stabilizing rates|Moderate refi recovery|

|**Market Condition**|**Typical CPR**|
|---|---|
|Extremely slow market|3–6 CPR|
|Normal market|8–15 CPR|
|Strong refinance environment|20–35 CPR|
|Extreme refinance wave|40–70+ CPR|

# **3. COVID Refinance Boom (2020–2021)**

This was one of the largest refinance/prepayment periods in modern mortgage history.

|**Metric**|**Approximate Numbers**|
|---|---|
|30-year mortgage rates|Fell below 3%|
|Agency CPRs|Frequently 25–45 CPR|
|Some high-incentive cohorts|50–70+ CPR|
|Refinance share of originations|~65–75%|
|Total mortgage originations (2021)|~$4.5 trillion|
|Refinance originations|~$2.7–3.0 trillion|

Fannie Mae benchmark CPR examples:

- April 2021:
    - ~27.7 CPR for 30-year cohort  
- August 2021:
    - ~19 CPR after slowdown  

---

# **4. 2022–2023 Collapse in Refinancing**

When the Fed aggressively increased rates:

|**Metric**|**Approximate Outcome**|
|---|---|
|Mortgage rates|~3% → 7%+|
|Refinance volume decline|~80–90% collapse|
|CPR speeds|Fell into single digits|
|Mortgage banker layoffs|Massive industry contraction|
|MSR valuations|Increased sharply|

This was one of the fastest collapses in refinance activity historically.

# **6. Early Payoff (EPO) Numbers at Lender Level**

Exact EPO statistics are usually proprietary.

However, across wholesale and correspondent lending:

|**Metric**|**Typical Industry Range**|
|---|---|
|Early Payoff window|First 6 months|
|Severe EPO concern|First 90–180 days|
|EPO rates in stable market|~0.5–2%|
|Elevated refinance environments|~3–6%+|
|Aggressive broker channels|Higher variability|

Lenders care because EPOs can trigger:

- investor repurchase pressure
- premium recapture
- hedge losses
- servicing losses.

---

# **7. Why EPOs Became a Huge Topic During 2020–2021**

During ultra-low rates:

|**Issue**|**Impact**|
|---|---|
|Borrowers refinanced repeatedly|Rapid loan churn|
|Brokers “churned” borrowers|Regulatory scrutiny|
|MSRs prepaid rapidly|Huge servicing losses|
|Gain-on-sale assumptions broke|Margin pressure|
|Investors demanded tighter controls|Stronger monitoring|

This led to:

- stricter EPO clauses
- broker scorecards
- recapture campaigns
- loyalty/refi retention programs.

# **10. Most Important Insight**

Historically, prepayment behavior is dominated by just three things:

|**Driver**|**Importance**|
|---|---|
|Rate incentive|Highest|
|Housing turnover|Very high|
|Borrower behavior|Extremely difficult to model|

That is why:

- prepayment modeling
- MSR valuation
- EPO prediction
- refinance propensity modeling

became some of the most sophisticated quantitative areas in mortgage finance.
