## Basic Sequential Tranches

1. **Sequential Pay (A, B, C tranches)** - Plain vanilla structure where principal flows in orderachievable+1
    
2. **Z-Bond (Accrual tranche)** - Receives no payments until all other tranches mature; interest accruesvaamllc+1
    

## Structured Tranches (Prepayment Protection)

3. **PAC (Planned Amortization Class)** - Protected payment schedule using a PSA bandrbcwealthmanagement+1
    
4. **TAC (Targeted Amortization Class)** - Single PSA target, protects only against fast prepaymentsbusiness.purdue+1
    
5. **Companion/Support tranches** - Absorb volatility to protect PAC/TAC bondshudwayglass+1
    

## Interest Rate Tranches

6. **Floaters** - Coupon rate adjusts upward with an index (e.g., SOFR + spread)cliffsnotes+1
    
7. **Inverse Floaters** - Coupon rate moves inversely to the index (higher rates = lower coupon)ramurapt.wordpress+1
    
8. **Fixed-Rate tranches** - Standard fixed coupon[capitalmarkets.fanniemae](https://capitalmarkets.fanniemae.com/media/4396/display)
    

## Principal-Only and Interest-Only Tranches

9. **PO (Principal-Only)** - Receives only principal payments, no interestramurapt.wordpress+1
    
10. **Super PO** - Hypersensitive PO tranche with extreme prepayment exposure[business.purdue](https://business.purdue.edu/faculty/mcconnell/publications/Valuation-and-Analysis-of-Collateralized.pdf)
    
11. **IO (Interest-Only)** - Receives only interest payments, no principal[ramurapt.wordpress](https://ramurapt.files.wordpress.com/2009/10/lehman-floating-rate-cmos-2004.pdf)
    
12. **Inverse IO** - Interest-only tranche with inverse floating coupon[ramurapt.wordpress](https://ramurapt.files.wordpress.com/2009/10/lehman-floating-rate-cmos-2004.pdf)
    

## Hybrid and Specialty Tranches

13. **Residual tranches** - Receives leftover cash flows after all other tranches are paid[cliffsnotes](https://www.cliffsnotes.com/study-notes/16932052)
    
14. **VADM (Very Accurately Defined Maturity)** - Even tighter prepayment bands than PAC[sfu](http://www.sfu.ca/~rjones/econ482/readings/DBGMresearch_2003_CMO_intro.pdf)
    
15. **Jump Z-tranches** - Z-bonds that can "jump" to receiving payments under certain conditions[sfu](http://www.sfu.ca/~rjones/econ482/readings/DBGMresearch_2003_CMO_intro.pdf)
    

## Complex Structures

CMOs can have **hundreds or even thousands** of tranches in a single deal, mixing and matching these types. For example, you could have a PAC floater, a sequential inverse floater, or a companion Z-bond.achievable+3
