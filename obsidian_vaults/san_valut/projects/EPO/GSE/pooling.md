## Who Does What

**Lenders (Originators):**

- Banks and mortgage companies originate loans to borrowers
    
- They sell these mortgages to Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac, or investment banks
    
- Once sold, they're done—prepayment risk is transferred away
    

**GSEs and Investment Banks (The Pooling Side):**

- They buy thousands of individual mortgages and pool them together
    
- They create CMOs (Collateralized Mortgage Obligations) by slicing the pool into tranches[hudwayglass](https://hudwayglass.com/house/24371+VERDANT+SQ,+Farmington+MI)
    
- They sell these tranches to investors (pension funds, insurance companies, hedge funds)
    
- **This is where PAC, TAC, Z-bonds, and sequential structures matter**[hudwayglass](https://hudwayglass.com/house/24371+VERDANT+SQ,+Farmington+MI)
    

## Why They Do This

Different investors have different risk appetites. Some want stable, predictable cash flows (they buy PAC tranches), while others accept volatility for higher yields (they buy support tranches or Z-bonds). The GSEs structure these tranches to redistribute prepayment risk so they can sell to a broader investor base.[hudwayglass](https://hudwayglass.com/house/24371+VERDANT+SQ,+Farmington+MI)

**Bottom line:** Lenders don't worry about this complexity—it's the GSEs and Wall Street structurers who design these tranches to manage and sell prepayment risk to institutional investors.
